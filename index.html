About

Bio

I’m an emerging technical writer living in the Portland, Oregon area. I pursued technical writing because I can simplify complex concepts. I moved to Portland with my partner and our three cats in 2025. While volunteering with the Woodlawn Neighborhood Association as a technical writer, I helped them modernize their documentation infrastructure. I collaborated with the WNA board to design an accessible documentation system using universal design principles.

Through my education and volunteer work, I gained experience with documentation practices like single-sourcing methodology and docs-as-code systems. I hold a bachelor’s degree in English as well as a certificate of Technical Writing. I enjoy writing about a variety of topics, be it prose, policy, or products. I’m interested in opportunities to create accurate, consistent, and accessible user-facing content.

Mission statement

Technical writing is the process of creating documentation that anticipates human needs. A technical writer must combine an understanding of language, editing, accessibility, design, and orchestration to anticipate user’s needs. My goal is to create accessible and user-centered documentation that helps organizations communicate effectively. I hope that my work will help people find the information they need from your organization.
